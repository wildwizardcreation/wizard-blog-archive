Anonymous asked:

If you don't mind I also have questions about your art? 1. What brush(s) do you use? And 2. What do your layers look like?

🥭

i dont mind at all 🥭!! i'll take any opportunity i can to shill for my favorite brush in the whole wide world: jens claessens' The Best Brush aka his chalk brush, which i use a modified version of for every single part of my painterly process (sketching, flats, rendering, detailing, etc. this thing is a fucking beast) though i prefer the triangle brush for "pencil sketch" stuff. like my gale anime girl art! the dylpixel circle brush with no pressure is what i call my "mspaint" brush, and its what i use to doodle and/or lay down really quick ideas the quick lasso eraser is my only eraser and my god. once you beat the learning curve theres no going back from this thing 🥴

wiz tip: i dont use ANY sizing pen pressure with ANY of my brushes. i have a hotkey for adjusting the brush size by dragging and i hiiiiighly recommend that method and/or a preset brush size system for any painterly styles.

as for layers, my process usually involves as few layers as possible! (cause i focused a Lot on traditional methods while shifting from an illustrative style to painterly) when im making big adjustments, or moving along the rendering pipeline in a noticeable way, ill often make a new layer to paint ontop of only so i can switch it on and off to check if the previous 'version' looked better for any particular reason. but as soon as im ready to move on i merge it with the layer below and repeat those steps. (that's why my progress photos are laid out Like That. i take screenshots at various points to show my wife cause without them im left with the final portrait on a single layer and like... the background color layer sdkfsjkd)
