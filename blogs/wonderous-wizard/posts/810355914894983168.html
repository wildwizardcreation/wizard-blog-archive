in terms of my process i promise there's very little mystery to it all skdhfjs the biggest differences are my works that use direct refs (screenshots, photography) vs works that use semi-direct refs (3d models) vs works where i hit the ground running and hope for the best

direct ref works:

direct ref works are easy enough. im a big ol cheater (color picking asshole) so i lay down flats in big easy to adjust shapes, and build up/down from there. my biggest painting sin (ESPECIALLY in direct ref work) is kinda building the planes in reverse, where i start with darks or midtones and build into lighter tones, rather than the other way around (like traditional painters do). this is obviously fine but it'd just make transitioning into traditional work x10 harder if i ever wanted to try

semi-direct works:

semi-direct works im shyer abt just cause i have a Thing with acknowledging that i use 3d models in some of my work sigh HOWEVER!!! secondary to my painting work, i'm a 3d artist! the above is a decent example of me using it in a photobash-y sense, but below are examples of my model for my avatar oc newom (next to painted work using him as a ref), a render for a durge/gale painting that i got bored with, and a finished ultraman headsculpt im planning on 3d printing and sticking on my action figure!

'vibes only' works:

vibe only work is rare for me because i really struggle to convince myself it's worth finishing and/or posting like 90% of the time. but the best way to approach it is exactly how any veteran illustrator would instruct: loose lines, vague shapes that look like nothing, etc, until you've nailed down the movement and/or tone you're looking for and then go in with actual intent.

i also use this method for figuring out the vibes of larger semi-direct ref work when i have an idea and need it down asap (though a LOT of those are lost to canvas clearing, over painting, and/or are entirely illegible 😭)
